At Each 30-Minute Visit

1. Milk Sample

  • Amount: 10 mL (about 2 teaspoons)
  • Collection: By hand expression or your own pump
  • Timing: Morning preferred (6-10 AM)
  • No special preparation needed

2. Blood Sample

  • Amount: 7.5 mL (1-2 small tubes)
  • Method: Standard arm blood draw
  • Similar to routine blood test
  • Takes 2-3 minutes
  • Performed by trained medical staff

3. Brief Questionnaire (10-15 minutes)

  • Current health status
  • Medications and supplements
  • Feeding patterns
  • Baby's growth and health
  • Lifestyle factors

4. Simple Measurements

  • Your weight
  • Vital signs (blood pressure, etc.)

What We Measure

In your milk: FGF23 protein, vitamin D, other factors

In your blood: FGF23, hormones, vitamin D, minerals

About the Study

Q: What is FGF23? ‚ñº
A: FGF23 is a protein that helps regulate minerals and bone health. We're investigating if it's present in breast milk and what role it might play in infant development.
Q: Why is this study important? ‚ñº
A: This is the FIRST study to look at FGF23 in human milk. Your participation helps us understand how breast milk supports infant bone and organ development, especially important for preterm babies.

About Participation

Q: Will this affect my baby's medical care? ‚ñº
A: Absolutely not. This is research only. Your baby's medical care is completely separate and will not be affected in any way.
Q: Can I participate if I supplement with formula? ‚ñº
A: Yes! Mixed feeding is perfectly acceptable as long as you can provide the required milk samples.
Q: What if I'm taking medications? ‚ñº
A: Standard prenatal vitamins and calcium/vitamin D supplements are fine. Some medications affecting metabolism may exclude you, but we'll review your specific medications during screening.
Q: Can I reschedule visits if needed? ‚ñº
A: Yes! We have flexible time windows (¬±3 to ¬±7 days) and can work around your schedule.

About Privacy & Safety

Q: How is my information protected? ‚ñº
A: All your data is:
  • Pseudonymized (no names on samples)
  • Encrypted (secure digital storage)
  • GDPR-compliant (European privacy standards)
  • Accessible only to authorized research team
Q: What are the risks? ‚ñº
A: Minimal risks:
  • Blood draw: Minor discomfort, possible small bruise
  • Milk collection: No additional risk beyond normal pumping
  • Time: 90 minutes total over 3 months
Q: Can I withdraw from the study? ‚ñº
A: Yes, at any time, for any reason, with no consequences.
